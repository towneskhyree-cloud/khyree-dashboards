// Lesson 01

The rich don't work for money.

Money obeys those who control fear and greed. The poor & middle class let fear (of bills) and greed (of more stuff) drive their decisions — that's why they stay on the treadmill.

+
"The poor and middle class work for money. The rich have money work for them."

The Core Principle

A raise doesn't make you rich — it just makes the trap bigger. More income usually means more spending (a bigger house, nicer car, more debt). The actual move is to learn how money works so you stop being its servant. Your mind is your most powerful asset; train it before you train your bank account.

What To Memorize

  • Fear and greed run the poor. "I need this job or my family starves" = fear. "If I had more money, I'd be happy" = greed. Both keep you trapped.
  • Most people have a price. They take any job that pays more, regardless of whether it builds them. Don't let your salary cap your imagination.
  • Emotions are the lock. Knowledge is the key.

Action Items

  • Sit with the fear instead of running from it. Ask: "What am I avoiding by chasing this paycheck?"
  • Track every dollar that comes in this month. Where does it actually go? (Most people are surprised.)
  • Replace "I can't afford it" with "How can I afford it?" — the first kills creativity, the second activates it.
For Khyree

You stopped working for money the day you started selling systems. The $97/mo recurring is money working while you pray Fajr.

// Lesson 02

Why teach financial literacy?

School teaches academic and professional literacy — never financial. So adults with PhDs go broke from buying liabilities they think are assets. Rule #1: Know the difference between an asset and a liability. Buy assets.

+
"An asset puts money in your pocket. A liability takes money out of your pocket."

The Core Principle

Most people can't define "asset" correctly. They call their house, their car, their 401k assets. Rich Dad's test: which direction does the money flow? If it flows OUT every month, it's a liability — no matter what the bank or the realtor calls it. Wealth is built one rule at a time: every dollar you earn buys an asset that earns more dollars.

What To Memorize

  • Asset = puts $ IN your pocket. Examples: businesses you don't run yourself, real estate that cash-flows, stocks, bonds, royalties, IP that earns passive income.
  • Liability = takes $ OUT of your pocket. Examples: your mortgage, car loan, credit cards, and yes — your primary residence.
  • The story of your wealth lives in your balance sheet, not your income statement. Big paycheck + big liabilities = poor. Small paycheck + growing assets = rich.
  • The Holy Grail: when income from your asset column exceeds your monthly expenses → you're financially free.

Action Items

  • Write your personal balance sheet TODAY. Two columns: Assets and Liabilities. Be honest. Most people's house goes in the wrong column.
  • Calculate your "freedom number" — total monthly expenses. That's the income your asset column needs to generate before you're free.
  • Every month, ask one question: did I add to my asset column or my liability column?
  • Stop buying liabilities that look like assets (status purchases dressed up as investments).
For Khyree

Each $97/mo Khyree City contract is also an asset (recurring revenue). Your freedom number stated: $3K/week ($12K/mo). Goal: build the asset column to throw off $12K monthly without requiring your active labor.

// Lesson 03

Mind your own business.

Your profession pays the bills. Your business builds your wealth. Don't confuse them. Most people work hard at someone else's business their whole life and wonder why they never built one of their own.

+
"Financial struggle is often the result of people working all their life for someone else."

The Core Principle

"Mind your own business" doesn't mean quit your job. It means: keep the day job, but spend every spare hour building YOUR asset column. Your salary feeds your business — the businesses owned by others get your loyalty for 8 hours, your business gets the other 16.

What To Memorize

  • Your profession ≠ your business. Ray Kroc said McDonald's wasn't in the burger business — it was in the real estate business. The burgers paid for the property.
  • Real assets to acquire: businesses that don't require your presence, income-producing real estate, stocks, bonds, notes/IOUs, royalties from IP, anything that has value/produces income/appreciates.
  • Don't quit your day job. But don't let your day job define your wealth ceiling.

Action Items

  • List the assets you currently own. Are any of them paying you monthly without your active labor?
  • This week: identify ONE asset you can acquire in the next 90 days (digital product, content library, rental, ownership stake).
  • Schedule 90 minutes/week labeled "build my business" — non-negotiable, no client work allowed in that slot.

// Lesson 04

The history of taxes & the power of corporations.

Tax was invented to take from the rich. The rich responded by inventing the corporation — a legal entity that lets them earn → spend → pay tax (instead of earn → tax → spend like everyone else). Same dollar. Different order.

+
"Knowledge is power. With money comes great power that requires the right knowledge to keep it and make it multiply."

The Core Principle

The tax code is written FOR business owners and investors, not against them. It's full of incentives — to start companies, to hire, to build buildings, to invest in equipment. Employees don't get to use most of these. The order of operations is everything: the corporation pays expenses with PRE-TAX dollars; the employee pays expenses with POST-TAX dollars.

The Four Pillars Of Financial IQ

  • Accounting. Read financial statements. Spot strengths and weaknesses of any business.
  • Investing. The science of money making money. Strategy, not gambling.
  • Markets. Understanding supply and demand. What people will pay, when, and why.
  • The law. Tax code, corporation structures, asset protection, securities law. Free education from books and accountants.

Action Items

  • Form an LLC or S-Corp this quarter if you haven't already. Khyree City revenue should NOT flow through your personal account.
  • Hire a tax-strategist accountant (not a tax-filer). The good ones save you 5–10x what they cost.
  • Read one book per quarter on the tax code or business structures. Compounding knowledge.
  • Track every business expense religiously. What feels like a personal cost might be deductible.
For Khyree

Get a US-based accountant who understands expat/Section 911 (Foreign Earned Income Exclusion: ~$120K/yr earned abroad can be tax-free). Your Cairo flights = business travel. Your laptop = equipment. // Lesson 05

The rich invent money.

Opportunities are not born — they're created by people who can see what others can't. While most people wait for the "right" stock or property to drop, the rich are building deals, structures, and businesses out of thin air.

+
"Often in the real world, it's not the smart who get ahead, but the bold."

The Core Principle

Two types of investors: (1) those who buy packaged investments — picks the broker offers, the rental on Zillow, the index fund. (2) Those who CREATE investments — they see a deserted lot and imagine a salon suite, see a barber and imagine an empire. The second type uses creativity, courage, and financial IQ to manufacture deals where others see nothing.

The Five Things That Hold People Back

  • Fear. Fear of losing money is real. The rich don't fear failing — they manage fear. They start small, learn fast, and stay in the game.
  • Cynicism. "What if the market crashes?" "What if it doesn't work?" Doubt is paralysis dressed up as wisdom.
  • Laziness. The busiest people are often the laziest financially. Too "busy" to study money, structure deals, build assets.
  • Bad habits. "Pay yourself first" — buy assets BEFORE paying bills. The pressure of unpaid bills sharpens your hustle.
  • Arrogance. "I already know" = the door slams shut. The rich stay students forever.

Action Items

  • This month, look at one situation in your life and ask: "What asset can I create here that doesn't exist yet?"
  • Pay yourself first. Set up an automatic transfer the day money hits — to investments/assets BEFORE bills.
  • Identify your biggest fear about money. Name it. Write it down. Then attack it with knowledge.
  • When you don't know something, say "I don't know" and learn — not "that's stupid" and dismiss.
For Khyree

Inventing money is your superpower. You looked at no-website barbers and saw $797 setup fees. You looked at your father's struggling suites and saw a system. You looked at 111K dormant followers and saw a community. Most people would walk past all three. Don't let the cynicism creep in when 14 cold calls in a row hang up — that's the test of lesson 5.

// Lesson 06

Work to learn, don't work for money.

Take jobs for the skills they teach, not the salary. The highest-paying job in your 20s might be the worst thing for your 40s if it teaches you nothing transferable.

+
"The main management skills needed for success are: management of cash flow, management of systems, and management of people."

The Core Principle

Most people specialize too early. A lifetime in one field makes you the expert nobody can replace — until that industry shifts and your skills don't transfer. Become a generalist who knows a little of everything: sales, marketing, accounting, systems, leadership, public speaking. These are the skills that print money in any market.

Skills To Build (Memorize This List)

  • Sales. The #1 skill of every wealthy person. If you can't sell, you can't get paid.
  • Marketing. How to make people want what you have before you ever speak to them.
  • Cashflow management. Reading the numbers. Knowing where money comes from and where it goes.
  • Systems thinking. Designing processes that run without you in the room.
  • People management. Leading, hiring, firing, motivating.
  • Communication. Writing, speaking, persuading, negotiating.

Action Items

  • Audit your current skills. Which of the six above are you weakest in? Spend 30 days obsessively on that one.
  • Take a job (or project) that pays less but teaches more — only if the skill is transferable to your wealth-building.
  • Read one book/month in a skill OUTSIDE your specialty.
  • Find a mentor who's 10 years ahead of you in the skill you need most. Buy them dinner. Ask questions.

/ 02 — The Vocabulary

Eight terms to know cold.

Get these right and you'll think clearer than 95% of people. Use them in conversation — they reshape how you make decisions.

01Asset
Anything that puts money INTO your pocket without your active labor.
A rental property. A book that pays royalties. A business with a manager running it.
02Liability
Anything that takes money OUT of your pocket — even if it has value.
Your house with a mortgage. Your car. Your boat. Things that bleed you monthly.
03Cash flow
The direction money moves between income, expense, asset, and liability columns.
Salary in → expenses out → broke. Asset in → asset in → free.
04Financial IQ
The combined skill of accounting, investing, markets, and law. The four pillars.
Knowing whether to take the W-2 job or start the LLC. That decision IS financial IQ.
05Pay yourself first
Move money to assets BEFORE paying bills. The pressure forces you to find more income.
Money hits → 10% to investments AUTO → then rent → then everything else.
06Rat race
The trap where higher income just creates higher expenses. More raises = more debt.
Promotion → bigger house → bigger mortgage → still broke at month 30.
07Doodads
Kiyosaki's word for liabilities people THINK are assets. Status purchases dressed up.
Luxury car, designer wardrobe, second home you don't rent. All doodads.
08Financial freedom
When monthly income from your asset column exceeds your monthly expenses.
$12K/mo expenses + $13K/mo from rentals & royalties = free, regardless of any job.
/ 03 — Mindset Gap

The two scripts playing in people's heads.

Same situation, two different reactions. The first one is what most people say. The second one is what builds wealth.

Poor Dad's Script

What keeps you stuck

  • "I can't afford it."
  • "Money is the root of all evil."
  • "Study hard so you can find a good company to work for."
  • "I'm not interested in money."
  • "The reason I'm not rich is because I have you kids."
  • "Don't take risks."
  • "Our home is our greatest investment."
  • "Pay your bills first."
  • "It's hard to get ahead in this economy."
  • "I'll never be rich."
Rich Dad's Script

What builds wealth

  • "How can I afford it?"
  • "The lack of money is the root of all evil."
  • "Study hard so you can find a good company to BUY."
  • "Money is power. Learn how it works."
  • "The reason I MUST be rich is because I have you kids."
  • "Learn to manage risk."
  • "Our business is our greatest investment."
  • "Pay yourself first."
  • "This economy creates new opportunities."
  • "I'm a rich man, and rich people don't do this."

/ 06 — The Four Pillars Of Financial IQ

The subjects school never taught.

Master these and the game changes. Stay ignorant in any one of them and the others can't save you.

A

Accounting

Read financial statements. Spot the strengths and weaknesses of any business in 60 seconds. Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement — these three documents tell every story.

I

Investing

The science of money making money. Strategy not gambling. Knowing the difference between speculation, value investing, and cash-flow investing — and choosing on purpose.

M

Markets

Supply and demand. What people want, what they'll pay, when, why, and what makes price move. The market doesn't care about your feelings — but it rewards those who read it well.

L

Law

Tax code, corporate structure, asset protection, contracts, securities. Free education through books, podcasts, and a good accountant. The wealthy aren't smarter — they just know the rules.

The book in one sentence.

Use your job to fund your asset column, learn the four pillars cold, structure everything through a corporation, and remember — wealth is measured in the freedom your money buys you, not the things it buys you.
