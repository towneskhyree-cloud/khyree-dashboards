01 // PILLAR ONE

Accounting.

Financial literacy. The ability to read the numbers and know what they're telling you.

DEFINITION

Accounting is reading the story the numbers tell.

Every business — whether it's a barber chair, a salon suite, or Apple — speaks one language: three financial statements.

The Income Statement tells you if you made money this month. Income minus expenses.

The Balance Sheet tells you what you own versus what you owe right now. Assets minus liabilities = net worth.

The Cash Flow Statement tells you where the actual cash moved. Often different from "profit" — and this difference kills businesses.

02 // PILLAR TWO

Investing.

The science of money making more money. Knowing what's a deal versus what's a trap.

DEFINITION

Investing isn't gambling. It's a science with formulas.

An investor doesn't ask "will this go up?" — that's a gambler. An investor asks: "how much cash flow does this asset produce, what did it cost, and what's my return?"

The core formula is ROI — Return on Investment. Money made / money put in = your return percentage. Anything less than the bank pays in interest? Bad investment.

Kiyosaki splits investments into 4 classes: businesses, real estate, paper assets (stocks/bonds), and commodities (gold, oil). Most rich people pick one or two and master them. They don't dabble.

WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE

The investor's question, every single time.

THE ROI CALCULATION
Annual cash flow from asset$X
Money you put in$Y
ROI = X ÷ Y × 100= __%
EXAMPLE — FILLING ONE EMPTY SUITE
Cost: marketing 1 suite$200
Result: 1 tenant signs at $200/wk$10,400/yr
ROI5,200%

That's why Cut Creaters' single best "investment" right now isn't real estate or stocks — it's marketing dollars to fill suites. The ROI dwarfs everything else.

CUT CREATERS APPLICATION
→ APPLY IT TO CUT CREATERS

Every dollar has to fight for its job.

Before you spend on anything, ask: "What's the ROI?"

  • $500 on Instagram ads to fill 2 suites = $20K/yr return = 4,000% ROI
  • $2,000 redesigning the lobby = $0 direct return = 0% ROI (delay it)
  • $300 on a referral bounty (pay tenants $300 to refer a tenant) = if 1 fills = 3,400% ROI
  • $5,000 buying the building? Not yet — fix the income statement first, then talk acquisition
THE COMMON MISTAKE
⚠ DON'T DO THIS

Investing in things that feel like investments but don't produce cash. A nicer car. A bigger house you live in. A course you don't finish. Kiyosaki's line: "Your house is not an asset" — because it takes money out your pocket every month, not in.

03 // PILLAR THREE

Understanding
Markets.

Supply and demand. Why something's worth what it's worth — and when that worth is about to change.

DEFINITION

A market is a conversation between supply and demand.

Two forces, always: how much of the thing exists (supply) and how badly people want it (demand). When demand is up and supply is down, prices rise. Reverse it, prices fall.

Understanding markets means knowing what people actually want (not what you think they should want) and what they're willing to pay. Most businesses fail because the founder built something nobody asked for.

The advanced version: knowing market timing — when a market is about to expand or collapse before everyone else sees it.

WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE

Reading a market in 4 questions.

THE MARKET DIAGNOSTIC
1. Who is the customer?
→ specific person, not "everyone"
2. What's the pain?
→ what are they losing without you?
3. What do they pay alternatives?
→ that's your price ceiling
4. Why you over them?
→ if no answer, no business
CUT CREATERS APPLICATION
→ APPLY IT TO CUT CREATERS

100 empty suites = market failure. Find the why.

The market is telling you something. Your job is to listen, not argue.

  • Customer: Atlanta stylists/barbers earning $800-$2,500/wk who want their own space
  • Pain: Booth-renting at someone else's shop, no privacy, no brand, owner taking a cut
  • Alternatives: Sola Salons, Phenix, MY Salon Suite — call them, learn their pricing + waitlist
  • Why Cut Creaters? 30-yr brand. Decatur location. Pop's reputation. Find the angle nobody else has.

Field research week 1: tour 3 competitor properties pretending to be a tenant. Note their price, amenities, vibe, fill rate. That's market research.

THE COMMON MISTAKE
⚠ DON'T DO THIS

Falling in love with your offer instead of the customer. Cut Creaters has been "we have suites" for 30 years. The market shifted — Atlanta now has competitors with better lighting, on-site beauty supply, content rooms for IG. The market doesn't owe you tenure. What does it want now?

04 // PILLAR FOUR

The Law.

The rules of the game. Tax law, corporate structure, contracts. The rich know them. The poor don't.

DEFINITION

The same dollar can be taxed at 40% or 0% — depending on what you know.

Law is the part of financial IQ most people skip — and it's the most expensive thing they can skip. Two people earning the same income can keep wildly different amounts based on their legal structure, tax strategy, and contracts.

Three sub-pillars sit under this one:

Tax law: What income is taxed how, what's deductible, what's depreciable. A business owner's expenses come out before tax. An employee's expenses come out after tax. That alone is a 30-40% advantage.

Corporate law: LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp. Each has different tax treatment, liability protection, and exit options. Wrong structure = lose your house if the business gets sued.

Contract law: Tenant agreements, vendor contracts, IP protection. Your mom learned this the hard way with Loc N Gel. The formula was real. The protection wasn't.

WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE

Same income. Two different worlds.

EMPLOYEE — $100K SALARY
Gross income$100,000
Tax (federal + state, ~30%)($30,000)
After-tax money$70,000
Living expenses($60,000)
LEFT TO INVEST$10,000
BUSINESS OWNER (LLC) — $100K REVENUE
Revenue$100,000
Business expenses (deductible)($60,000)
Taxable profit$40,000
Tax (~25% on profit)($10,000)
KEPT$30,000

Same $100K. The employee keeps $10K to invest. The business owner keeps $30K. That's not a small difference — that's a 3x compound advantage every single year.

CUT CREATERS APPLICATION
→ APPLY IT TO CUT CREATERS

Three legal questions to answer in the first 30 days back.

  • Entity check: Is Cut Creaters an LLC? S-Corp? Sole proprietorship in Pop's name? Each has different tax + liability implications. Get the answer from the CPA.
  • Tenant agreements: Are tenants on written leases? Month-to-month? Can a tenant skip without paying? Every empty suite that walked owing money is a contract failure.
  • Loc N Gel lesson: Mom's product was stolen because the formula wasn't IP-protected. When her relaunch happens (Year 2), it gets a trademark, an LLC, and a non-disclosure on day one. Never again.

Action: Get a small-business CPA on a $200/mo retainer. They'll save you 5-10x that in tax + structure mistakes.

THE COMMON MISTAKE
⚠ DON'T DO THIS

Treating "the law" as paranoia or paperwork. The legal structure of a business is the financial chassis — wrong chassis, wrong outcome no matter how good the engine. Most family businesses run on a handshake until the handshake fails.
