Not a brand story. A working document. Five short chapters on where this chair came from, what it stands on, and how a forty-five minute haircut becomes the most expensive luxury in the room.

01
CH · ORIGIN

The first cut happened
at nine years old.

The clippers were borrowed. The chair was a kitchen stool. The client was a cousin who needed a lineup before school. The cut was uneven. The cousin paid in three dollars and a handshake. The business began that afternoon.

Two decades later, the clippers are no longer borrowed. The chair sits in a 24,000 sq ft building in Decatur. The lineup is precise. The ticket has gone up. The work has not changed.

"Cutting since I was nine. Refining ever since."

02
CH · CRAFT

The cut is forty-five minutes.
The ceremony is the rest.

Anyone who has held clippers for ten years can deliver the cut. The difference is what happens around it. The welcome by name. The cold towel before the cape. The five-question consultation that earns the upcharge before the first pass. The hot towel at the finish. The photograph before the cape lifts. The handwritten note in their pocket on the way out.

Total added cost: under two dollars. Total added time: under five minutes. Tip lift over twelve months: 35–60%. Word-of-mouth referral becomes the entire acquisition channel.

FIG · B-02 — CEREMONY · ELEVATION T-5 → T+45
03
CH · CHAIR

One chair.
By design.

Most barbershops run on volume — six chairs, eight chairs, walk-ins from open to close, no two visits the same. The Khyree City chair is singular by design. One operator. One calendar. One client at a time. No upsell from a stranger at the front desk.

The trade-off is honest. The calendar is smaller. The ticket is higher. The client is remembered. Three lines recorded in the notebook after every cut — something personal, something technical, something to follow up on. Six weeks later, the door opens, and you're called by name.

"You aren't a haircut. You're a notebook entry, a slot on Thursday, and a hand on the door when you leave."
04
CH · HOUSE

Inside a 30-year building.
In Decatur.

Cut Creaters opened in 1996. Twenty-four thousand square feet. One hundred and forty private suites. Opened by Omar and Danielle Townes and run continuously by them since the doors first unlocked. The Khyree City chair sits inside, holds one of the suites, and operates under the house standard.

The building has its own gravity. Forty independent barbers, stylists, braiders, and beauty operators run their own books out of the same address. A neighborhood of craft, under one roof.
